Freedom Fighters

NAME:Mahatma Gandhi

Mahatma Gandhi is well-known as a freedom fighter and the father of our nation. ... He was born in Porbandar, Gujarat on October 2, 1869. His father's name was Karamchand Gandhi, and his mother's name was Putlibai Gandhi. Gandhiji was married to Kasturba Kapadia.

  1. Nonviolent Resistance (Satyagraha):Gandhi developed and popularized the concept of nonviolent resistance, known as "Satyagraha," as a powerful tool for social and political change. His philosophy of nonviolence inspired many civil rights and freedom movements worldwide.
  2. Indian Independence: Gandhi played a pivotal role in India's struggle for independence from British colonial rule. His leadership in various movements, such as the Salt March and Quit India Movement, contributed significantly to India gaining independence onAugust 15, 1947.

NAME:Bhagat Singh

Bhagat Singh was a prominent Indian freedom fighter who played a significant role in the struggle for India's independence from British colonial rule. He is known for his passionate speeches and writings that inspired many during the freedom movement. One of his most famous speeches is the one he delivered during his trial in the Lahore Conspiracy Case in 1930. Here is an excerpt from that speech: "Inquilab Zindabad!

  1. Central Legislative Assembly Bombing: Bhagat Singh, along with Batukeshwar Dutt, threw non-lethal smoke bombs in the Central Legislative Assembly in Delhi on April 8, 1929, to protest against oppressive British laws. This daring act was aimed at raising awareness of the need for India's independence and drew significant attention to the cause.
  2. Hunger Strikes in Jail: While in prison, Bhagat Singh and his fellow inmates launched hunger strikes to demand better treatment and rights for political prisoners. These hunger strikes were significant because they garnered widespread support and sympathy for the imprisoned revolutionaries, both in India and internationally. Bhagat Singh's resolve during these hunger strikes showcased his determination and commitment to the cause of Indian independence.

NAME:Lokmanya Tilak

Lokmanya Tilak was born on July 23, 1856, in a small village inMaharashtra. From a young age, he showed remarkable intellect and a deep sense of responsibility towards his country. He believed firmly in the power of education and enlightenment as a means to uplift society. Tilak was not only a scholar but also a fearless advocate for India's independence. His famous slogan,"Swaraj is my birthright, and I shall have it," encapsulates his unwavering commitment to the cause. He used his writings and journalism to awaken the masses and ignite the flame of nationalism in their hearts.

  1. Promotion of Swadeshi and Boycott Movement: Lokmanya Tilak was a strong advocate for the Swadeshi (self-reliance) and Boycott movements during the struggle for Indian independence. He played a pivotal role in popularizing the concept of boycotting British goods and encouraging the use of indigenous products. His efforts in this regard contributed to the economic self-sufficiency of India and promoted the idea of self-reliance among the Indian population.
  2. Founder of the Home Rule Movement: Tilak was one of the founders of the All-India Home Rule League in 1916, along with Annie Besant. The Home Rule Movement aimed to demand self-governance for India within the British Empire. This movement significantly mobilized public opinion and support for the cause of self-rule, laying the foundation for later mass movements and political developments that ultimately led to India's independence.
